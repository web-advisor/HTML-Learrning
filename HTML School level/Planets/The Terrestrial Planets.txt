<p>
The Terrestrial Planets builds the INNER SOLAR SYSTEM.There are FOUR Terrestrial Planets.These planets have dense, rocky compositions, few or 
no moons, and no ring systems.</p>

<br>
<font size="6" face="showcard gothic">
The Terrestrial Planets
</font>

<br>
<img src="5.jpg" width="90%" height="80%">
<br>

<p>
 They are composed largely of refractory minerals, such as the silicates, which form their crusts and mantles, and metals, such as iron and nickel,
 which form their cores. Three of the four inner planets (Venus, Earth and Mars) have atmospheres substantial enough to generate weather; all
 have impact craters and tectonic surface features, such as rift valleys and volcanoes. The term inner planet should not be confused with inferior
 planet, which designates those planets that are closer to the Sun than Earth is (i.e. Mercury and Venus).
</p>
